Dira Parent — Privacy Policy

Dira Parent is built around a simple idea: what you enter about your family is yours, and it should stay that way by default. This page explains exactly what the app does and doesn't do with your information, in plain language.

What stays on your device, always

By default, Dira Parent stores everything locally, on your own device — your tasks, your check-ins, which situations you've visited, all of it. None of this is sent to any server we operate. If you never create an account or connect Google Calendar, nothing about your family ever leaves your phone.

Your check-in history is automatically deleted from your device after 90 days on a rolling basis. You can export a copy as a CSV file at any time before then, from the Your Month screen.

If you create an account (optional)

Creating an account is never required to use the app. It exists only for people who want their tasks and check-ins to follow them to a new device or survive a reinstall. If you choose to create one:

  • We store the email and password (or equivalent credential) needed to sign you back in.
  • We do not sell this information, and we do not share it with advertisers or data brokers.
  • You can request deletion of your account and associated data at any time — see Contact, below.

If you connect Google Calendar (optional)

Connecting Google Calendar is a separate, optional choice from creating a Dira account — you can do one without the other. If you connect it:

  • Dira requests read-only access to your calendar, so events can be shown inside the app. Dira does not create, edit, or delete anything in your actual Google Calendar.
  • The access token is stored securely on your device. Your calendar data is retrieved directly from Google and displayed to you — it does not pass through or get stored on any server we operate.
  • You can disconnect Google Calendar at any time from within the app, which removes the stored token.

What we don't do

As of this policy's writing, Dira Parent does not display ads, does not use third-party advertising or tracking services, and does not sell personal information to anyone, under any circumstances.

The Get Help Now page

The crisis and safety resources page includes contact information for services we don't operate, such as 911, the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, Childhelp, and Crisis Text Line. If you reach out to one of these, your interaction with them is covered by their own privacy practices, not this one.

Dira Parent is not a substitute for professional or medical care. The app offers gentle, everyday support — not therapy, diagnosis, or emergency services. If you're worried about your child's safety or your own, please use the resources on the Get Help Now page or contact emergency services directly.

Children's privacy

Dira Parent is intended for use by parents and caregivers — adults — not by children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in a way that matters, we'll update the date at the top of this page. Continuing to use the app after a change means you've had a chance to review it.
